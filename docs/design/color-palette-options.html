Design discussion · issue #13

Grayscale doesn't read as calm — it reads as unfinished

Right now every color token in the app is pure gray (zero chroma). The brief asks for calm and legible, no gamified pressure — but calm needs a color to be calm in. Three directions below, each shown as the actual bottom nav, stat card, input, and button from the app, in both light and dark. Nothing here is implemented — this is for picking a direction before it goes into #13.

A

Dusk

A muted periwinkle-violet. Reads reflective and unhurried — closer to dusk light than a brand color. Plus: carries no "success/fail" baggage the way green does, so a missed day never looks like a wrong color. Watch for: violet is an easy default for "calm wellness app" right now — it needs the muted, deep treatment shown here, not a bright lavender, or it drifts generic.

Sage

A dry, dusty green — closer to sage leaf than gym-app lime. Plus: the most legible "health app" signal of the three; nobody will misread the category. Watch for: green carries the strongest built-in "good/bad" coding of any hue — even used as a single neutral accent, a green number can read as "on track" and an unfilled one as "behind," which cuts against the brief's no-judgment stance more than it first appears to.

C

Pond

My suggestion. A muted slate-teal — still, clear water rather than forest or lavender field. Plus: shares green's "living, steady" association (and the water/ turtle connection is a nice quiet bonus) without green's pass/fail coding — teal doesn't read as "correct" the way a green checkmark does. Also keeps this app visually distinct from life-kaleidoscope's warm paper palette, so the two don't feel like the same app reskinned. Watch for: the least "obviously health-app" of the three — a deliberate trade for feeling calmer and less generic.

My take

Pond (C), muted

Between purple and green, I'd actually steer away from both defaults. Green is the reflex choice for anything health-adjacent, which makes it the hardest of the three to make feel specific to this app rather than "a" fitness app — and its built-in good/bad coding sits uneasily next to a brief that explicitly bans failure-state red and guilt copy. Purple is calmer and judgment-free, but it's become the default shorthand for "mindful app" broadly (meditation, journaling, sleep) — Pond gets the same calm without either association, and the water/turtle read is a small bonus, not the reason for it.

If you'd rather go with A or B instead, or want a fourth direction, say the word — this is just my read, not a lock-in.

Nothing on this page touches the repo. Once a direction is picked, the exact tokens (mapped to the app's existing --primary / --ring / --muted variable names, shown above) go into issue #13 as part of its scope — implementation happens from there, not from this page.
